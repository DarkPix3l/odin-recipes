Farinata of Chickpeas

Farinata is a savory, thin pie with a golden crust, made using only four simple ingredients. This dish has ancient origins and is a specialty of Liguria, particularly the city of Genoa. It consists of a batter made with chickpea flour, water, olive oil, and salt. Traditionally baked in a wood-fired oven, this recipe adapts the process for a home oven! Though simple to prepare, a few key steps are essential for success: allowing the batter to rest (to fully hydrate the chickpea flour) and generously greasing the pan to prevent sticking. Chickpea farinata is not only loved in Liguria but also known by other names in different Italian regions, such as "cecina", "socca", or "fainé".
In Livorno, it’s famously called "5 and 5" and often served in a sandwich. Whether you enjoy it as a standalone dish, an appetizer, or paired with cheeses and cured meats, farinata is a testament to authentic Italian street food. And you, how do you like it best?

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Preparation time: 15 min
  • Cooking time: 25 min
  • Serves: 8 people
  • Cost: Low
  • Note: recommended resting time: at least 6 hours

INGREDIENTS

FOR 3 TRAYS (32 CM DIAMETER)

  • 300 g chickpea flour
  • 50 g extra virgin olive oil
  • 900 g water
  • 10 g salt
  • Black pepper, as needed

TO GREASE

  • 70 g extra virgin olive oil
