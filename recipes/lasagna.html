Artichoke Lasagna Recipe

Artichoke lasagna is a rich and appetizing dish, a vegetarian alternative to classic baked lasagna—prepared without the traditional Bolognese sauce. The cheeses and velvety béchamel ensure a luscious, stringy texture, while the artichokes, with their sweet and slightly bitter taste, add their distinct and unmistakable flavor. This special white lasagna, made with winter vegetables, is enriched with inviting aromas and flavors. Next Sunday, switch up your menu and serve artichoke lasagna—a seasonal first course that’s also perfect for Easter lunch!

  • Difficulty: Easy
  • Preparation time: 20 min
  • Cooking time: 45 min
  • Serves: 6
  • Cost: Low

INGREDIENTS

FOR A 20x30 CM BAKING DISH

  • Dry egg lasagne - 250g
  • Scamorza (provola) - 400g
  • Grana Padano DOP, grated - 250g
  • Extra-virgin olive oil - 4 tablespoons
  • Salt - to taste
  • Artichokes - 12
  • Taleggio - 200g
  • White wine - 1 glass
  • Shallot - 1
  • Black pepper - to taste

FOR THE BÉCHAMEL

  • Whole milk - 1l
  • Butter - 100g
  • Salt - to taste
  • Flour (Type 00) - 80g
  • Nutmeg - a pinch
